Poi Mochi

A Hawaiian Twist on a Japanese Dessert

My family has been making mochi for generations, as a Japanese tradition to bring in every New Year. Japanese mochi can be made a few different ways, but our favorite is chichi dango. Making and eating mochi brings my and many other local families lots of joy and memories every year and it's made mochi one of my favorite desserts.

Poi is a revered Hawaiian food made from the root of the kalo(taro) plant. Kalo is believed to be the very first ancestor of Hawaiians, which makes it sacred and special to the Hawaiian people. It can be enjoyed with many different types of foods and recpes.

A few years ago, my mom and I were cooking at my aunty's house and craving something sweet. My aunty had leftover poi in the fridge and we thought it was the perfect opportunity to try making poi mochi. It was delicious and sweet, and now we make it every time we get together.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound bag of poi
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 1/2 cup sugar
  • 3 1/2 cup mochiko flour
  • Frying oil of choice

Instructions

  1. Heat cooking oil of choice in frying pan until it reaches 350-375 degrees F.
  2. Combine poi, water, sugar and mochiko flour until well conbined.
  3. Using a spoon or cookie scooper, scoop about 1 tablespoon of batter and drop in oil (I recomment doing about 10 at a time).
  4. Fry for about 10-12 minutes until outside of batter is golden brown.
  5. Remove poi mochi from oil and place on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  6. Enjoy!
