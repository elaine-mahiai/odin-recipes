Shoyu Chicken

My Favorite Hawaiian Style Shoyu Chicken Recipe

Shoyu chicken is a recipe that's been passed down through generations in Hawaii for decades. With just four ingredients, it's an easy-to-make but delicious to eat meal that satisfies a savory craving.

I've tried shoyu chicken from dozens of different places but my favorite will always be from my mom's kitchen. The recipe is simple but effective and I can't wait for you to all try it.

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds frozen bone-in chicken thighs with skin on
  • 1 cup shoyu (soy sauce)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • Few slices of ginger

Tip: This recipe tastes best with Aloya shoyu, but you can use low sodium kikoman if it's not available.

Steps

  1. Add shoyu and water to a large pot and heat on medium-high heat until sugar dissolves.
  2. Add chicken thighs and ginger to the pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Once boiling, bring heat down to medium and cover the pot.
  4. Cook covered for 10 minutes then flip chicken and cook for another 10 minutes.
  5. Flip again and cook for 20 minutes. Repeat once more for 20 minutes.
  6. Optional: Remove one cup of liquid from the pot and pour into a small saucepan. Add 1 tablespoon of cornstarch and mix until cornstarch is dissolved and sauce is thickened. Pour thickened sauce over chicken when serving.
  7. Enjoy with rice!
