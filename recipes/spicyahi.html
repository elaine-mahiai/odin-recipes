Spicy Ahi

My Favorite Spicy Ahi Recipe

Poke is a traditional Hawaiian dish eaten on its own or as an appetizer. Poke means "cubed" in Hawaiian so this dish is literally just cubed fish. Throughout the years, locals have made all kinds of different poke recipes with Japanese influence. Various ingredients have been added and mixed in and it's now a dish enjoyed worldwide.

Spicy Ahi poke uses a spicy creamy sauce and is best served on its own or with rice. This recipe is my favorite variation but feel free to experiment and make it your own!

Ingredients

  • 16 oz sashimi grade ahi tuna cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1/3 cup mayo (Kewpie or Best Foods taste best)
  • 4 tablespoons sriracha
  • 2 teaspoons shoyu (soy sauce - Aloha or Kikkoman)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon masago
  • 2 green onions finely chopped
  • Furikake

Tip: You can use any shoyu you have but Aloha Shoyu tastes the best.

Instructions

  1. Combine mayo, sriracha, shoyu, sesame oil, and masago. Stir until smooth.
  2. Add cubed ahi tuna and toss to coat.
  3. Refrigerate for 15 minutes or until ready to serve.
  4. Serve over rice and add furikake and onions to garnish.
  5. Enjoy!
